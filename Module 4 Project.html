Eiji Aonuma (Japanese: 青沼 英二, Hepburn: Aonuma Eiji, born March 16, 1963) is a Japanese video game designer, director, and producer at Nintendo. He is a senior officer within their Nintendo EPD division and serves as the producer of the The Legend of Zelda franchise.

Career
Aonuma was born as Eiji Onozuka[2] on March 16, 1963, in Nagano Prefecture, Japan[3][4]. Graduating from the Tokyo University of Arts in 1988, he achieved his masters degree in composition design, working on marionettes[5][6]. After graduation and meeting with select individuals, he was interviewed by Nintendo where he met Shigeru Miyamoto and shortly thereafter landed the job[7]. Having never done programming before, there were some that thought Aonuma was way out of his league. He was quick to prove them wrong, however, when he started working on videogame graphic design by creating sprites for various games such as 1991's NES Open Tournament Golf.[7]

After Aonuma's success with programming sprites, Shigeru Miyamoto recruited Aonuma to join the development of The Legend of Zelda series, where he excelled.[7] He began work on The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time., and its sequel, The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask. Upon completion of The Legend of Zelda: The Wind Waker, Aonuma considered moving on to other projects, but was convinced by Miyamoto to continue with the series.[8] Since then, he has led the production of The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess[9], Phantom Hourglass[9], Spirit Tracks[9], Link's Crossbow Training[9], Skyward Sword[9], A Link Between Worlds[10], Breath of the Wild[11], and Tears of the Kingdom.[12]

Aonuma plays percussion as a founding member of the Wind Wakers, a brass band formed in 1995. The band comprises over 70 Nintendo employees who perform a few concerts a year.[13][14][15] At the 2016 Golden Joystick Awards, Aonuma received a lifetime achievement award, and was later promoted to deputy general manager within the company's Entertainment Planning & Development division in June 2019.[16][17] In May 2023, at an interview about The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom on Nintendo's official website, it was shown that Eiji Aonuma was promoted at some point from deptuty general manager to senior officer of the same division.[18]
